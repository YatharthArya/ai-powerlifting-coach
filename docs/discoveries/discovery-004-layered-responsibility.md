## Layered Responsibility and Constitutional Governance

**Date:** Phase 0 – Day 28

**Status:** 🟡 Under Review

### Confidence Architecture

- Confidence may exist independently at multiple stages of the evidence lifecycle.
- Different architectural objects may require independent confidence models.
- Decision justification may be distinct from knowledge confidence.
- Consequences of action and inaction may influence decision justification independently of evidence confidence.

### Constitutional Governance

- Expertise improves the quality of contributions but never replaces constitutional validation.

### Communication Philosophy

- Communication may adapt to user communication needs without altering scientific content, evidence, uncertainty, confidence, or recommendations.


## Current Review Focus

Current architectural discussions are primarily investigating:

- The architecture of confidence across the evidence lifecycle.
- The distinction between confidence, prediction, and decision justification.
- The influence of uncertainty, consequences, context, and risk on justified action.
- The relationship between evidence, confidence, communication, and decision-making.
