# Discovery 002

**Date:** Phase 0 – Day 25

**Title:** Multi-Dimensional Quality Framework

**Status:** 🟡 Under Investigation

## Summary

The concept of "quality" appears throughout the platform's methodology but cannot be accurately represented as a single metric or score.

Instead, quality appears to emerge from multiple independent dimensions that collectively influence the reliability, confidence, and usefulness of information as it progresses through the evidence lifecycle.

This suggests that every major stage of the methodology may require its own quality model rather than relying on a universal definition of quality.

---

## Initial Observations

Current discussions suggest that quality exists at multiple stages of the methodology, including but not limited to:

- Observation Quality
- Measurement Quality
- Context Quality
- Information Quality
- Evidence Quality
- Validation Quality
- Decision Quality
- Knowledge Quality

Each quality dimension appears to be evaluated using different criteria while contributing collectively to the overall confidence of the decision-making process.

---

## Working Hypothesis

Overall decision quality is not determined by a single quality metric.

Instead, decision quality emerges from the interaction of multiple quality dimensions throughout the evidence lifecycle.

Higher-quality observations, measurements, context, evidence, validation, and knowledge increase the likelihood of producing higher-quality decisions.

---

## Early Discussion Points

Current discussions suggest that quality may depend upon multiple characteristics rather than any single variable.

Potential characteristics include:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Consistency
- Completeness
- Precision
- Validity
- Context Availability
- Traceability
- Reproducibility
- Transparency

These characteristics are considered hypotheses and require further architectural investigation before becoming permanent components of the platform.

---

## Open Research Questions

- How should each quality dimension be formally defined?
- Should every stage of the evidence lifecycle have an independent quality model?
- Which quality dimensions influence confidence?
- Which quality dimensions influence uncertainty?
- How should quality be represented computationally?
- Can quality be quantified without oversimplifying complex evidence?
- How should different quality dimensions interact during decision generation?

---

## Potential Architectural Impact

If validated, this discovery may influence the design of:

- Evidence Lifecycle
- Evidence Engine
- Decision Engine
- Knowledge Engine
- Database Architecture
- Machine Learning Architecture
- Confidence and Uncertainty Models

---

## Current Status

This discovery has **not** yet been validated.

It is preserved as a research hypothesis and shall remain outside the permanent architecture until it has undergone sufficient challenge, refinement, and validation through future architectural discussions.
